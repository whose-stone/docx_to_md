**OPERATING AGREEMENT**
**OF**
**BLUEBYTES LLC**
A Florida Limited Liability Company

This Operating Agreement (“Agreement”) is entered into effective as of February 22, 2026, by and between:

**Bruce-Alan Barnard**, an individual
**S. Brandon Michael Barnard**, an individual
(collectively, the “Members”).
The Members hereby agree as follows:

**ARTICLE I**
**Formation**
**1.1 Name**
The name of the Company is **BlueBytes LLC** (“Company”).
**1.2 Formation**
Articles of Organization have been filed with the Florida Department of State pursuant to Chapter 605, Florida Statutes.
**1.3 Principal Office**
The principal office shall be determined by the Members.
**1.4 Term**
The Company shall exist perpetually unless dissolved pursuant to this Agreement.

**ARTICLE II**
**Purpose**
The purpose of the Company is to develop, own, license, operate, and maintain artificial intelligence platforms, digital knowledge systems, software applications, and related technology services providing legal information, research tools, and educational resources to law enforcement officers, police legal advisors, governmental agencies, and related professionals, including search and seizure law and other legal aspects of law enforcement operations, and to engage in any lawful business permitted under Florida law.

**ARTICLE III**
**Capital Contributions and Ownership**
**3.1 Initial Contributions**
The Members’ initial capital contributions are set forth in **Schedule A**, attached hereto.
**3.2 Membership Interests**
Each Member owns a fifty percent (50%) Membership Interest.
**3.3 Additional Contributions**
No Member shall be required to make additional capital contributions absent a written agreement.

**ARTICLE IV**
**Management**
**4.1 Member-Managed**
The Company shall be managed by its Members.
**4.2 General Authority**
Each Member may bind the Company in the ordinary course of business within their designated operational domain as set forth below.

**ARTICLE V**
**Defined Operational Domains and Tie-Breaking Authority**
Because the Members possess specialized expertise in distinct areas of Company operations, authority shall be allocated as follows:

**5.1 Legal Content Domain**
Final decision-making authority in the event of disagreement shall rest with **Bruce-Alan Barnard** regarding:
- Legal content development
- Constitutional interpretation
- Search and seizure analysis
- Curriculum design
- Legal training materials
- Legal disclaimers
- Substantive legal risk positioning
- Government legal engagement strategy

**5.2 Technology and Platform Operations Domain**
Final decision-making authority in the event of disagreement shall rest with **S. Brandon Michael Barnard** regarding:
- Software architecture
- AI model selection and configuration
- Infrastructure and hosting
- Data engineering
- DevOps
- Security systems
- Technical vendor selection
- Platform deployment and scaling

**5.3 Major Decisions Requiring Unanimous Consent**
The following require unanimous written consent:
- Admission of new Members
- Issuance of additional equity
- Incurrence of debt exceeding $5000
- Sale of substantially all assets
- Merger, restructuring, or conversion
- Amendment of this Agreement
- Sale or exclusive license of core intellectual property
- Dissolution

**5.4 Good Faith Limitation**
Tie-breaking authority shall be exercised in good faith and consistent with fiduciary duties under Florida law.

**ARTICLE VI**
**Intellectual Property; Reversion; Third-Party Beneficiary Protections**
**6.1 Company Ownership**
All intellectual property developed or acquired in connection with Company business, including but not limited to software code, AI systems, model weights, prompts, datasets, training materials, trademarks (including BLUEBYTES), domain names, documentation, trade secrets, and goodwill, shall be the sole property of BlueBytes LLC during its existence.

**6.2 Assignment by Members**
Each Member irrevocably assigns to the Company all intellectual property created within the scope of Company activities.
Each Member agrees to execute additional documentation as reasonably necessary to perfect Company ownership.

**6.3 Restriction on Individual Exploitation**
No Member may independently commercialize or exploit Company intellectual property without unanimous written consent.

**6.4 Intellectual Property Reversion Upon Dissolution**
Upon dissolution of BlueBytes LLC, and after satisfaction of all lawful debts and obligations, all remaining intellectual property of the Company shall automatically and irrevocably transfer to:

**Vstars US Incorporated**, a Florida corporation, or its legal successor.
Such transfer includes all:
- Software code
- AI models and training artifacts
- Proprietary datasets
- Trademarks and goodwill
- Domain names
- Documentation
- Derivative works
No Member shall retain any individual claim to such intellectual property following dissolution.

**6.5 Third-Party Beneficiary**
Vstars US Incorporated is expressly designated as an intended third-party beneficiary of Sections 6.4 and 6.6 and shall have independent legal standing to enforce those provisions.

**6.6 Restriction on Amendment**
Sections 6.4 and 6.5 may not be amended, waived, or modified without:
- Unanimous written consent of the Members; and
- Written consent of Vstars US Incorporated.
Any attempted amendment without such consent shall be void.

**6.7 Survival**
This Article shall survive dissolution of the Company.

**ARTICLE VII**
**Allocations and Distributions**
**7.1 Profits and Losses**
Allocated 50% to each Member.
**7.2 Distributions**
Made at times determined by unanimous consent.
**7.3 Tax Classification**
The Company shall be taxed as a partnership unless otherwise elected.

**ARTICLE VIII**
**Restrictions on Transfer**
No Member may transfer any Membership Interest without the express written consent of the other Member.

**ARTICLE IX**
**Death, Disability, or Withdrawal**
Upon death, disability, or voluntary withdrawal, the remaining Member shall have the option to purchase the departing Member’s interest at fair market value determined by independent appraisal.

**ARTICLE X**
**Dispute Resolution**
Disputes outside defined domains shall first be submitted to mediation in Florida. If mediation fails, binding arbitration under AAA rules shall apply.

**ARTICLE XI**
**Indemnification**
The Company shall indemnify Members to the fullest extent permitted under Florida law for actions taken in good faith within the scope of authority.

**ARTICLE XII**
**Miscellaneous**
This Agreement shall be governed by Florida law. If any provision is found unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall remain in effect.
